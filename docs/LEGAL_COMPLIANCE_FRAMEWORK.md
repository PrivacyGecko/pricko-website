## 5. Utility-First Framing Guidelines

### 5.1 Always Lead with Utility

**DO**:
1. Describe what the token DOES
2. Explain functional benefits
3. Show how it integrates with products
4. Highlight specific use cases

**DON'T**:
1. Lead with token economics
2. Focus on scarcity or supply limits
3. Emphasize secondary market trading
4. Discuss price history or predictions

## 6. Securities Law Compliance

### 6.1 Howey Test Avoidance

The Howey Test determines if something is a security. To avoid classification:

**Criteria 1: Investment of Money**
- ‚úÖ Frame as "purchase of utility," not "investment"
- ‚úÖ Use language: "acquire," "obtain," "purchase for use"
- ‚ùå Avoid: "invest," "invest in," "capital contribution"

**Criteria 2: Common Enterprise**
- ‚úÖ Emphasize decentralized governance
- ‚úÖ Highlight community-driven development
- ‚ùå Avoid: "team will increase value," "company profits"

**Criteria 3: Expectation of Profits**
- ‚úÖ Focus on utility value and service access
- ‚úÖ Describe functional benefits only
- ‚ùå Avoid: "profit from price increase," "returns," "appreciation"

**Criteria 4: Efforts of Others**
- ‚úÖ Emphasize token holder governance and participation
- ‚úÖ Describe ecosystem as community-driven
- ‚ùå Avoid: "team will make you money," "sit back and profit"

### 6.2 Safe Harbor Compliance Checklist

For each piece of content, verify:

- [ ] No promise or expectation of profit
- [ ] No reliance on efforts of others for value increase
- [ ] Utility clearly described and functional
- [ ] Token used for access, governance, or services
- [ ] No investment-related language
- [ ] Risk disclosures present and prominent
- [ ] Clear that token value may fluctuate or go to zero

## 7. Jurisdiction-Specific Considerations

### 7.1 United States (SEC)

**Key Requirements**:
- All disclaimers must be present
- No offers to US persons without proper registration (unless utility token exemption applies)
- Clear "Not Financial Advice" statements
- Risk warnings required

**Approved Language**:
```
Token availability varies by jurisdiction. US persons should verify compliance
with local regulations before purchasing. This is not an offer or solicitation
in any jurisdiction where such activity is restricted or prohibited.
```

### 7.2 European Union (MiCA Regulation)

**Key Requirements**:
- Consumer protection disclosures
- Clear risk warnings
- Transparent fee structures
- Right to information

**Approved Language**:
```
For EU residents: Privacy Gecko complies with Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA)
regulations. Full risk disclosures, fee structures, and consumer rights information
are available in our [Token Documentation].
```

**Place on all pages mentioning token sale**:

Token availability and compliance requirements vary by jurisdiction. Verify local
regulations before purchasing. Privacy Gecko makes no representations regarding
the lawfulness of token acquisition in any specific jurisdiction.
